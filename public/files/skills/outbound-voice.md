# Outbound Voice — Cold Email & LinkedIn DM Voice System

## When to use
Use when writing cold outbound emails, LinkedIn connection requests, LinkedIn DMs, or any first-touch message to someone who doesn't know you. Applies to B2B sales outreach, partnership requests, investor outreach, or any context where you're initiating a conversation with a stranger.

## Voice Rules

### Tone
- All lowercase. No capitalization except proper nouns (company names, people names) where needed for clarity.
- Casual. Like a smart person in your network reaching out organically.
- Direct. Say what you mean in the fewest words possible.
- Specific numbers over vague claims. "99.47% fill rate" not "industry-leading accuracy."
- Confident but not arrogant. You're sharing something useful, not begging for attention.

### Structure
- Open with organic context. Why are you reaching out to THIS person right now? ("saw you follow X", "noticed you're building Y", "your post about Z caught my eye")
- One specific, relevant proof point in the body. Not three. One.
- End with a question, not a pitch. ("are you using...?" / "curious what you're running now?" / "can i send more info?")
- Short. Under 100 words for email. Under 300 characters for LinkedIn connection requests. Under 3 sentences for LinkedIn DMs.

### What to avoid
- No salesy language, ever. No "excited to share", "love what you're building", "game-changing", "cutting-edge."
- No em dashes (—). Hyphens are okay if natural. Commas and periods for everything else.
- No exclamation marks.
- No "I hope this finds you well" or any variant.
- No "just following up" without adding new information.
- No "quick question" in subject lines unless you actually have a quick question.
- No walls of text. If the email needs a scroll, it's too long.
- No attachments in cold email. Links are okay if they add value.
- No fake personalization. ("I was impressed by your background in..." when you clearly weren't.)

### CTAs
- Always a question. Never a demand.
- Soft offers: "can i send more info?" / "want me to send the comparison?" / "would it be worth a quick look?"
- Curiosity questions: "are you using X?" / "what are you running for Y?" / "curious if this has come up?"
- Never: "Let's schedule a call" / "Are you free Thursday?" / "I'd love 15 minutes"
- The goal of email 1 is a reply, not a meeting. Meetings come from conversations.

### Sequence Design
- Email 1: Context + one value prop + question CTA
- Email 2: New angle or proof point + question CTA (don't repeat email 1)
- Email 3: Offer something concrete (free test, comparison, resource) + low-friction CTA
- Email 4 (breakup): Short. Respectful. Leave the door open. ("if timing changes: [email]")
- Each email should work standalone. Never reference "my last email."
- Spacing: Day 0, Day 2-3, Day 7, Day 14. Not daily.

### LinkedIn-Specific
- Connection requests: Under 300 characters. Context + who you are + why connecting. No pitch.
- DMs (after accept): Different angle from the connection request. Max 2-3 sentences. Ask a question.
- Never pitch in a connection request. The request is to connect, not to sell.

### Subject Lines
- Lowercase. Short. Specific.
- Good: "saw you follow crustdata" / "quick question on your enrichment stack" / "free head-to-head test"
- Bad: "Partnership Opportunity" / "Exciting News from [Company]" / "[First Name], let's connect!"
- A/B test 2 subject lines per email. One direct, one curiosity-based.

## Quality Check
Before sending, verify:
1. Is every sentence lowercase? (except proper nouns)
2. Zero em dashes?
3. Under 100 words? (email body)
4. Ends with a question?
5. Opens with organic context specific to the recipient?
6. Contains at least one specific number or proof point?
7. Zero salesy language?
8. Would you actually read this if a stranger sent it to you?
