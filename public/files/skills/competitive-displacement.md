# Competitive Displacement — Vampire Attack GTM Methodology

## When to use
Use when building a go-to-market campaign that targets a specific competitor's users/followers as your lead source. Works for any product category where you can identify people who use or follow a competitor and offer them something measurably better.

## The Vampire Attack Framework

### Concept
A "Vampire Attack" treats a competitor's audience as a pre-qualified lead list. These people already understand the problem space and are actively engaged with a solution. Your job is to show them a better one.

### Phase Structure

**Phase 1: Competitor Research**
- Deep analysis of competitor's product, pricing, positioning, customers, funding, team
- Map their strengths (acknowledge honestly) and weaknesses (your displacement angles)
- Identify their public audience (LinkedIn followers, Twitter followers, community members, event attendees)
- Document what they claim vs what they actually deliver

**Phase 2: Battlecard & Positioning**
- Side-by-side comparison on every dimension
- Identify 3-5 displacement angles where you win measurably
- Write honest "where they win" section (builds credibility)
- Create internal battlecard for sales team
- Define positioning angles for different personas

**Phase 3: Lists, Content & Lead Magnets**
- Extract and classify competitor's followers/audience
- Build exclusion list (competitors, investors, employees to skip)
- Score and tier targets by displacement potential
- Create ICP personas mapped to competitor's audience segments
- Write comparison content (blog, landing page, LinkedIn posts)
- Build lead magnet that doubles as proof of your superiority (bake-off kit, evaluation framework)

**Phase 4: Sequences & Nurture**
- Outbound sequences for competitor followers (high awareness, lead with displacement)
- Inbound nurture for people who find your comparison content (low-medium awareness, lead with education)
- Tier-based personalization (Tier 1: manual custom, Tier 2: segment templates, Tier 3: abbreviated)

**Phase 5: Sales Enablement**
- Discovery script with competitor-specific objection handling
- Demo guide optimized for competitive displacement conversations
- Follow-up templates including bake-off results delivery

**Phase 6: Launch Config**
- Tool setup (outbound platform, CRM, tracking)
- Link all placeholders to published assets
- Stagger launch by tier
- Define success metrics and feedback loops

### Key Principles

1. **Lead with displacement, not differentiation.** These people already use or evaluate your competitor. Don't educate them on the category. Show them what they're missing.

2. **Acknowledge competitor strengths honestly.** Saying "CrustData's company signals are solid" builds more credibility than pretending they don't exist. Your audience already knows the competitor. Don't insult their intelligence.

3. **The bake-off is the conversion mechanism.** Don't try to convince with words. Offer a head-to-head test on the prospect's own data. If your product is actually better, the data does the selling.

4. **Tier your effort by displacement potential.** Not every follower is worth a custom email. Score by: role relevance, company fit, seniority, likelihood of being an actual user vs casual follower.

5. **Messaging order matters.** For HIGH-awareness audiences (competitor followers): displacement angle first, differentiation second. For LOW-awareness audiences (inbound): education first, displacement later.

6. **Reusable system, not a one-off campaign.** Every phase produces templates and frameworks that work for the next competitor target. Change the competitor name, re-research, re-score, re-write. The structure stays.

### Displacement Scoring

Score each target on:
- **Role relevance (0-25):** Does their job involve using/buying the competitor's product type?
- **Company fit (0-25):** Would their company benefit from your specific advantages?
- **Seniority (0-15):** Can they make or influence a buying decision?
- **Consumer signal (0-15):** Do you have enrichment data that proves the displacement angle?
- **Company size (0-10):** Is the company big enough to be a real account?
- **Engagement (0-10):** How actively do they engage with the competitor?

Tiers:
- **Tier 1 (top 3-5%):** Manual 1-to-1 personalization. Custom research per target.
- **Tier 2 (next 20-25%):** Segment-level templates with merge fields. Full sequence.
- **Tier 3 (remaining):** Abbreviated sequence. Email only (no LinkedIn). 2-3 touches max.

### Content Strategy for Displacement

- **Comparison blog:** Long-form, SEO-targeted ("[Competitor] alternative", "[Competitor] vs"), honest side-by-side
- **Landing page:** Conversion-focused, comparison table, clear CTAs
- **LinkedIn posts:** Thought leadership that implicitly positions against competitor without naming them directly (except in specific competitive posts)
- **Lead magnet:** Evaluation framework or bake-off kit that's structured so your product wins on the highest-weighted dimensions

### Measuring Success

- Reply rate by tier and segment
- Bake-off request rate
- Bake-off → demo conversion rate
- Demo → customer conversion rate
- Which displacement angle generates the most positive replies
- Which email in the sequence drives the most conversions
- LinkedIn accept rate by connection request variant
