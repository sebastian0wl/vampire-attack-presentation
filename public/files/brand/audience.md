# Minerva BI — Audience Profile

## Primary Audiences

### Audience 1: Platform Buyer (Demand Generation Platform)

**Title:** CMO, CRO, VP Marketing, VP Sales, Head of Growth, Head of Performance Marketing

**Company profile:**
- Consumer-facing brands (DTC, luxury, real estate, financial services, hospitality, travel, sports/entertainment, subscription services)
- 50-5000 employees (sweet spot: 200-2000)
- US-based primarily, expanding
- Running paid media (Meta/Google), direct mail, or sales-driven acquisition
- Has a CRM (HubSpot, Salesforce) but struggles with data quality and segmentation

**Pain points (in their words):**
- "I spend $400K/mo on Meta and I genuinely have no idea who's seeing my ads."
- "We have 12 people on the marketing team and 8 of them just keep the data infrastructure running."
- "Our sales reps are calling leads who can't afford the product."
- "We tried a CDP implementation. It took 9 months and cost $250K."
- "Our match rates with our current enrichment provider are terrible — we can only use 20-30% of our file."
- "I don't have a unified view of my customer across channels."

**What they want:**
- Better ROAS on paid media without increasing spend
- Fewer, better leads for sales (quality over quantity)
- A way to route leads to the right sales rep by product fit
- A unified customer view without a 6-month implementation
- Direct mail that actually targets the right households

**How they buy:**
- Champion is usually VP/Director level who feels the pain daily
- Economic buyer is CMO/CRO who cares about pipeline and efficiency
- Evaluation period: 2-6 weeks for mid-market, 2-4 months for enterprise
- Proof of concept / pilot is typical before full commitment
- Compare against: existing tools (Meta native targeting, manual lead scoring), agencies, other enrichment providers

**Where they hang out:**
- LinkedIn (primary — thought leadership, peer recommendations)
- Industry conferences (Cannes Lions, SaaStr, DTC events)
- Newsletters (Morning Brew, The Hustle, CMO newsletters)
- Slack communities (Demand Curve, Pavilion, RevGenius)

### Audience 2: API Buyer (Consumer Intelligence API)

**Title:** CPO, VP Product, VP Engineering, Head of Data, Technical Founder

**Company profile:**
- SaaS platforms, martech tools, recruiting platforms, real estate tech, fintech
- Building consumer-facing features that need identity resolution or enrichment
- Technical team that evaluates APIs on performance, coverage, and docs
- 20-500 employees typically (growth-stage startups and scale-ups)

**Pain points (in their words):**
- "We tried Apollo and ZoomInfo — match rates are terrible on personal emails."
- "Our enrichment provider only covers B2B. We need consumer data too."
- "Building our own data pipeline would take 6 months and 3 engineers."
- "The API we're using has 200ms latency and it's killing our user experience."
- "We need income and wealth data but Experian wants a $500K annual minimum."

**What they want:**
- High match rates (70%+ on real customer files)
- Deep field fill (income, wealth, property, interests — not just name/email)
- Low latency API (<100ms)
- B2B2C coverage (personal AND professional identifiers)
- CCPA compliance baked in
- Simple integration (good docs, RESTful API, reasonable pricing)

**How they buy:**
- Technical evaluation first (API docs, sandbox, test file)
- Decision usually made by CPO or VP Product with eng team input
- Price sensitivity: moderate — they'll pay for quality data but compare unit economics
- Evaluation period: 1-3 weeks (run a test file, compare match rates)

**Where they hang out:**
- Twitter/X (developer and founder communities)
- Hacker News
- GitHub
- Product Hunt
- Technical newsletters (TLDR, Bytes)

### Audience 3: Agentic System Builders

**Title:** AI Engineer, Founder (AI-native startups), VP Product (at companies building agents)

**Company profile:**
- Building AI agents that interact with consumers on behalf of their users
- Need real-time consumer context to make good decisions (who to contact, what to say, how to prioritize)
- Using MCP, tool-use patterns, or custom agent frameworks
- Early-stage to growth-stage

**Pain points:**
- "Our agent is making decisions about consumers with zero context about who they are."
- "We need structured data that agents can reason over, not raw text."
- "Enrichment providers are built for dashboards, not for agent consumption."

**What they want:**
- Structured, machine-readable consumer profiles
- Real-time API with low latency
- MCP integration
- Scoring and triggers (not just raw data — actionable signals)

**Where they hang out:**
- Twitter/X (AI/ML community)
- Discord (AI builder communities)
- Anthropic forums, OpenAI community
- GitHub
