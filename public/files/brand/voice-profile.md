# Minerva BI — Voice Profile

## Jackson Engles (CEO) — Primary Content Voice

### Voice DNA

**Register:** Professional but not corporate. Jackson sounds like a smart friend who happens to run a data company, not like a press release. He's comfortable going deep on technical topics but always ties them back to "why should you care."

**Sentence structure:** Varies between short punchy statements and longer explanatory sentences. Not afraid of one-sentence paragraphs. Rarely uses em dashes. Almost never uses semicolons. Uses periods where most AI would use em dashes.

**Vocabulary:**
- Uses "the way to think about it is..." when framing concepts
- Says "that sort of thing" as a casual catch-all
- Says "like" as a conversational filler (in speech — remove for written content)
- Uses "really" for emphasis: "really really quite flexible"
- Uses "super" casually: "super helpful," "super general"
- Describes things as "pretty cool" or "pretty interesting"
- Doesn't use jargon without explaining it first
- Breaks down concepts by saying "so" at the start: "So the way it works is..."

**Tone patterns:**
- Confident without being arrogant. States facts directly, doesn't hedge.
- Gets excited about the technical differentiation (match rates, identity graph)
- Acknowledges competitors exist without trashing them
- Comfortable admitting what's not built yet: "there's a handful more that we're building"
- Uses specific numbers rather than vague claims: "469M individuals," "70-80% match rate"

**From actual Gong calls:**
- "What makes our identity graph different is two things. We have what's called a B2B2C identity graph."
- "A lot of the time they'll match on 20-30% of records as opposed to we'll match on 70-80%."
- "We basically built the context layer first. The product came after."
- "If somebody's come in to a form fill or something like that, what do you do with them?"
- "The platform's probably, like... I'll start at a higher level."

### What Jackson DOESN'T Sound Like:
- Corporate press release ("We are pleased to announce...")
- AI-generated LinkedIn post ("In today's rapidly evolving landscape...")
- Aggressive sales copy ("STOP wasting money on bad data!")
- Academic/formal ("It is imperative to understand that...")
- Self-deprecating to the point of undermining credibility

### Written Content Voice (LinkedIn / Blog)
When writing as Jackson for LinkedIn or blog content:
- First person singular ("I" not "we" for personal posts)
- Starts with a specific observation, number, or story — never a generic opener
- Gets to the point in the first 2-3 lines
- Backs up claims with specific numbers
- Ends with what comes next, not a motivational closer
- No hashtags in text body (if any, at the bottom separated)
- No emojis in the text
- Line breaks between every 1-2 sentences (LinkedIn formatting)

## Voice Rules (Apply to All)

1. Never use words from the AI kill list (delve, landscape, tapestry, pivotal, testament, etc.)
2. Never start with "In today's..." or "When it comes to..." or "It's worth noting..."
3. Maximum 1 em dash per 200 words
4. No rule-of-three lists unless the rhythm genuinely serves the point
5. No performed authenticity ("I'll be honest...", "Let me be real...")
6. Specific numbers over vague claims, always
7. Short sentences mixed with longer ones — vary the rhythm
8. End with something concrete, not a motivational platitude
