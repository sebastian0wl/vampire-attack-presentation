# Minerva BI — Positioning

## Primary Positioning

**The Context Advantage**
"Your CRM knows 20% of your customer. We know the other 80%."

This angle is unclaimed white space. No competitor owns the "missing consumer context" narrative. Windfall focuses on wealth data. Apollo/ZoomInfo focus on B2B contact data. Acxiom focuses on marketing data. Nobody frames themselves as the layer that fills the gap between what your CRM captures and what you actually need to know about your customer.

## Core Proof Points

1. **Match rate:** 70-80% vs industry standard 20-30%. Because we built a B2B2C identity graph that connects personal and professional identifiers. If someone submits a personal email, we still know their employer. If they submit a work email, we still know their home address.

2. **Data depth:** 2,168+ fields per person from 7+ vendor sources resolved into one unified graph. Demographics, financials, property records, professional data, behavioral signals, interests, household composition, vehicle ownership, political affiliation, donor behavior, and more.

3. **Population:** 270M+ Americans covered. 469M individual records. 499M PII records. 733M email records. 899M phone records.

4. **Implementation speed:** Agentic data engineer cuts CDP implementation from months to days. Automates the mapping of CRM/marketing/ad platform data into a unified schema.

5. **Real customer results:** Hard Rock Stadium (ICP scoring for season ticket tiers), Wander (paid media + direct mail optimization), Juicebox (enrichment powering recruiter search), Luxury Presence (homebuyer/seller scores in real estate CRM).

6. **Partnerships:** NBA, Experian, Clay. $20M raised across pre-seed/seed/Series A.

## What We Don't Say

- We don't trash competitors by name in public content (reference category, not company)
- We don't claim to be a CDP (we have CDP functionality but position as demand generation platform)
- We don't lead with "AI" — we lead with data, then show the AI layer on top
- We don't redact data provider names externally (use Provider A-D in partner-facing materials)
- We don't position as "just data" — always tie data to outcomes and workflows

## Competitive White Space Map

| Competitor Claims | What They're Missing (Our Opportunity) |
|---|---|
| Windfall: "Precise net worth data" | Only wealth data. No behavioral, no interests, no professional. Primarily nonprofit/fundraising focused. No platform for demand gen workflows. |
| Apollo/ZoomInfo: "B2B contact data" | B2B only. Can't match personal emails. No consumer context (income, property, interests). No CDP or workflow automation. |
| Acxiom: "Marketing data at scale" | Data breadth but no platform. No B2B2C graph. No self-serve. Expensive, enterprise-only, slow implementation. |
| Triple Whale: "Ecommerce analytics" | Ecommerce-only. No owned data asset. No enrichment beyond what ad platforms provide. |
| Hightouch: "Composable CDP" | Infrastructure-heavy, requires data engineering. No owned data. You bring your own enrichment. |
| Adobe/SFDC CDPs: "Enterprise marketing cloud" | Months to implement. Hundreds of thousands in consulting fees. Requires you to bring your own identity graph and enrichment data. |
| Crustdata: "Real-time B2B data for agents" | B2B focused. No consumer attributes (income, wealth, property, interests, household). Firmographic and professional data only. |
