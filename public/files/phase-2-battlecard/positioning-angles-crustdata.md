## Angle 1: "The Other 80%" [PRIMARY -- RECOMMENDED]

**Narrative:** CrustData gives your agent 90 fields about someone's job. Minerva gives your agent 2,168 fields about who they actually are — and even the professional fields are multi-sourced, standardized, and cleaned. If your agents interact with prospects and leads, you're building on 20% of the picture.

**Best for:** AI engineers and technical founders building agents that qualify leads, personalize outreach, score buying power, or route to product tiers. Anyone whose agent makes a decision about a person's likelihood to buy or ability to pay.

**Proof point:** 2,168 fields vs. 90 fields. 13 data categories vs. 2. Hard Rock Stadium uses the consumer layer to score season ticket buyers across product tiers. That scoring requires income, property, and behavioral data that no B2B-only API carries.

**How to deploy:**
- Lead with this angle in the LinkedIn connection request and first DM in the outbound sequence
- Use as the primary hook in the comparison content piece (LinkedIn post targeting CrustData followers)
- Use in the cold email subject line: "Your agent is working with 20% of the data"
- This is the angle for the one-pager headline and the battlecard opener

## Angle 2: "B2B2C Identity Resolution"

**Narrative:** Minerva's contact graph is best-in-class for professional AND personal emails, phones, and addresses. 733M emails, 899M phones, 270M+ addresses — all linked. Most teams are running a waterfall across 2-3 contact providers or just living with gaps. Minerva replaces the waterfall. One call, one vendor, most complete contact record in the market. CrustData can't match a personal email at all.

**Best for:** VP Product and CPOs at companies whose customer data mixes personal and professional identifiers. SaaS platforms with form fills, recruiting tools, fintech with consumer onboarding, or any product where users sign up with personal emails. Also teams currently paying for multiple contact data vendors or running expensive waterfalls.

**Proof point:** 70-80% match rate vs. 20-30% on mixed-identifier files. 733M email records. 899M phone records. Juicebox powers their recruiter search with Minerva's API, replacing multiple contact vendors with one integration.

**How to deploy:**
- Use as the follow-up angle when "The Other 80%" gets engagement but the prospect pushes back with "we're doing B2B"
- Deploy in the second email of the cold sequence as a proof-of-concept hook: "Send us a sample file with your personal emails, we'll show you what comes back"
- Use in LinkedIn comment replies when prospects mention match rate frustrations
- This is the angle that drives the head-to-head test CTA: "We're confident we're better. Run the test."

## Angle 3: "Your Agent Is Guessing"

**Narrative:** An agent that routes leads, sets prices, or personalizes offers without knowing income, property value, or buying behavior is guessing. CrustData tells your agent where someone works. Minerva tells your agent what they can afford.

**Best for:** Founders and AI engineers building agent-driven qualification, dynamic pricing, or personalized recommendations for consumer-facing products. Real estate tech, fintech, luxury brands, hospitality, subscription businesses.

**Proof point:** Hard Rock Stadium's ICP scoring routes leads to 100/200/300-level and suite products based on buying power. Wander uses enrichment data to optimize paid media targeting and direct mail for vacation property buyers. Both require consumer financial data that B2B APIs cannot provide.

**How to deploy:**
- Use in content marketing (LinkedIn posts, short-form technical content) aimed at the AI builder audience
- Deploy as the hook for case study distribution: "Here's how [customer] stopped guessing about buyer qualification"
- Works well in podcast/newsletter placements targeting technical founders
- Pair with a technical blog post showing the API response difference between a B2B-only call and a B2B2C call

## Angle 4: "One Call, Full Profile"

**Narrative:** To get consumer data alongside professional data, CrustData users need to add a second provider, a second integration, and stitch two responses together. Minerva returns everything in one API call. Professional data, consumer data, 2,168 fields, one endpoint.

**Best for:** Technical decision-makers who prioritize stack simplicity. VP Engineering, CPOs, and technical founders who are already managing multiple data vendors and want to consolidate.

**Proof point:** Juicebox replaced their enrichment stack with a single Minerva integration. Luxury Presence embeds Minerva scores directly in their CRM from one API call. No stitching, no second vendor.

**How to deploy:**
- Use as a supporting argument in the outbound sequence (email 2 or 3), not the lead angle
- Deploy in product-focused LinkedIn content: "We replaced 3 data vendors with 1 API call"
- Works as a closing argument when a prospect is comparing total cost of ownership across stacked providers vs. Minerva alone
- Include in the battlecard as a rebuttal to the "adding complexity" objection

## Angle 5: "Track Record vs. Promise"

**Narrative:** Minerva has $20M raised, named enterprise customers, and live partnerships with the NBA, Experian, and Clay. CrustData has a YC badge, $6M seed, and zero public case studies. When your data infrastructure depends on a vendor, shipped results beat accelerator logos.

**Best for:** Mid-market and enterprise buyers doing formal vendor evaluation. Procurement teams that need references, compliance documentation, and proof of scale. Also effective for technical founders who've been burned by early-stage vendors that disappeared.

**Proof point:** $20M vs. $6M. Five named customers vs. company names in product demos but no confirmed customer endorsements or published case studies. Three enterprise partnerships vs. none. CCPA compliant by design vs. unknown compliance status. G2/Capterra presence vs. none.

**How to deploy:**
- Use as a secondary reinforcement in later touches, not the lead angle (leads with social proof feel salesy)
- Deploy in the follow-up email after a match rate test: "Here's who else is using us"
- Include in proposal/evaluation documents when competing head-to-head
- Reference in objection handling when the prospect raises the "we haven't heard of you" concern

These people follow CrustData because they want B2B/professional data. They are currently buying or evaluating B2B data APIs. Lead with what they already care about (better B2B data), then expand to what they don't know they need (consumer data).

| Sequence Step | Primary Angle | Supporting Angle |
|---------------|---------------|------------------|
| LinkedIn connection request | **Our B2B Data Is Better Too** | -- |
| First DM / cold email | **Our B2B Data Is Better Too** (fill rates, standardization, verified phones) | One Call, Full Profile |
| Follow-up after engagement | One Call, Full Profile | B2B2C Identity Resolution |
